Target Policy Optimization
🔴 high AI_MODEL

What It Is

Target Policy Optimization (TPO) is a novel policy optimization method for reinforcement learning that separates the questions of which completions should gain probability mass and how the parameters should move to realize that change. This approach constructs a target distribution and fits the policy to it by cross-entropy, allowing for more efficient and stable learning. TPO is designed to improve upon existing methods like policy-gradient methods.

The Delta

Compared to LongRLVR, Target Policy Optimization (TPO) introduces a new approach to reinforcement learning by separating the questions of which completions should gain probability mass and how the parameters should move to realize that change. Unlike LongRLVR, which focuses on augmenting the reward signal to improve learning in long-context scenarios, TPO uses a target distribution to guide the policy update, allowing for more efficient and stable learning. Specifically, TPO constructs a target distribution q_i ∝ p_i^old \* exp(u_i) and fits the policy to it by cross-entropy, resulting in a loss gradient that vanishes once the policy matches the target. This approach enables TPO to outperform established methods like PG, PPO, GRPO, and DG on easy tasks and substantially outperform them under sparse reward, demonstrating its effectiveness in a broader range of scenarios.

Why It Matters

The practical significance of Target Policy Optimization lies in its ability to provide a more efficient and stable learning process, especially in scenarios with sparse rewards. By separating the questions of which completions should gain probability mass and how the parameters should move, TPO can avoid overshooting or undershooting, which can occur with standard policy-gradient methods. This can lead to improved performance in a wide range of reinforcement learning tasks.

Where To Use It

Here are some concrete use cases for Target Policy Optimization:
1. **Tabular bandits**: TPO can be used to improve the learning process in tabular bandits, especially in scenarios with sparse rewards.
2. **Transformer sequence tasks**: TPO can be applied to transformer sequence tasks, such as language modeling or text generation, to improve the efficiency and stability of the learning process.
3. **Billion-parameter LLM RLVR**: TPO can be used to optimize the policy in large-scale language models, such as those with billions of parameters, to improve their performance in reinforcement learning tasks.

What It Is

Shot-Based Quantum Encoding (SBQE) is a novel data embedding strategy for quantum machine-learning that distributes the hardware's native resource, shots, according to a data-dependent classical distribution over multiple initial quantum states. This approach enables a mixed-state representation whose expectation values are linear in the classical probabilities and can be composed with non-linear activation functions. SBQE is designed to overcome the bottleneck of efficient data loading in near-term quantum machine-learning.

The Delta

Compared to Learning Physical Operators using Neural Operators, Shot-Based Quantum Encoding (SBQE) differs in its application domain and the type of operators it learns. While the former focuses on learning physical operators for solving partial differential equations, SBQE is specifically designed for efficient data loading in quantum neural networks. The key innovation of SBQE is its ability to treat shot counts as a learnable degree of freedom, allowing for a mixed-state representation that can be composed with non-linear activation functions. This is in contrast to the physics-informed training framework of Learning Physical Operators, which decomposes PDEs using operator splitting methods and trains separate neural operators to learn individual non-linear physical operators.

Why It Matters

The practical significance of SBQE lies in its potential to improve the efficiency of data loading in quantum machine-learning, which is a major bottleneck in near-term quantum computing. By treating shot counts as a learnable degree of freedom, SBQE enables a more efficient and flexible data embedding strategy that can be composed with non-linear activation functions, making it a promising approach for quantum neural networks.
